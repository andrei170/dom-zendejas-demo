Where the math breaks

The lead pipeline is the missing layer.

To go from $15M to $30M, you do not need to double your hours and you do not need to become a creator. You need 14 more deals at your average ticket. At ~$30K take-home per deal, that math alone hits the entire $400K goal. The deals exist in your market every day. The system that surfaces them and routes them to your team does not.

Right now lead flow is cold prospecting + walk-ins + personal-network referrals. That is how you got to $15M. To get to $30M, the network has to scale faster than your time can scale, which means a system has to do it. Plus a recruiting layer so team capacity grows with deal flow, not behind it.

  • Pipeline: targeted homeowner outreach in your defined OC + LA market, routes to team intake
  • Recruiting: agent expansion layer so 3-agent team becomes 5+ as the deal flow demands it
  • Real estate stays the only thing you sell. No course identity shift required.

The build

Two pieces. One missing layer.

The lead pipeline that surfaces buyer/seller intent in your market every day, plus the recruiting layer that turns the aspiring-agent half of your DMs into team capacity. Both feed the same engine. Both ship inside one install. Course is FAQ-only, available later as a secondary stream when you want it.

Piece 1 · The Pipeline

Targeted SoCal homeowner sequence routing to your team intake.

Outbound sequence to homeowners in your defined OC + LA market, sized to your full ticket range ($700K starter homes to $3M+ luxury), not a single price band. Written for the homeowner who is "thinking about it, not in a rush". That is where the listing money actually lives. Sent from a deliverability-warmed Dom inbox, not a blast tool.

  • Multi-band targeting: public-record homeowner data filtered by tenure, equity, and ZIP cluster, segmented across $700K, $1M–$2M, and $3M+ tiers so each gets its own message
  • Sequence: 4–5 step email cadence per tier, "market check-in" tone (broker-to-owner), not pitch tone
  • DM triage layer: daily IG DMs sorted by intent, three buckets (see diagram below)
  • Reply routing: warm replies land in Follow-Up Boss with pre-qualification context attached
  • Reporting: reply rate by ZIP × tier, listing conversion, monthly review

Piece 2 · The Capacity

Agent recruiting layer turning daily DMs into team capacity.

Load-bearing precondition: recruiting agents under your team brand inside KASE Real Estate needs Keven Stirdivant's blessing before this piece ships. Standard team-lead structure (you recruit under your team within the brokerage license) usually works without friction, but we should align with KASE leadership before launch. The aspiring-agent half of your daily DMs is a recruiting funnel you currently send to free advice and competition. The same pipeline that captures buyer/seller intent in Piece 1 captures recruiting intent in Piece 2, so deal capacity grows with deal flow instead of behind it.

  • Recruiting page: "Join Dom's Team @ KASE" landing page (or full KASE-branded equivalent), the answer to send for aspiring-agent DMs
  • Application gate: filters serious from curious before any of your time is involved
  • Onboarding flow: day-one playbook, scripts, and lead-routing handoff so new agents start producing inside month one
  • Compounds: every new agent absorbs leads from Piece 1, freeing your calendar for higher-ticket deals
The realistic capacity math: new agents typically do $2–3M their first year while ramping (not the inflated $5M+ figures most coaches pitch). Two onboarded agents at $2.5M each = +$5M of team volume in year one, plus your override on their GCI layered on top of your personal book. Year two they produce closer to $5M each. The recruiting layer is a 3-year compound, not a year-one win. Real lift on the recruiting side starts in months 9–12 as the first cohort hits stride.
